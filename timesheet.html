Before Your First Paycheck - Set Up Direct Deposit

First-Time TAs: You need to set up direct deposit through MyU before your first pay period, otherwise your paycheck may be delayed or issued as a paper check. Take care of this as soon as you are onboarded.

To set up direct deposit:

  1. Go to myu.umn.edu and log in with your UMN Internet ID and password.
  2. Navigate to "My Pay" from the main dashboard or top menu.
  3. Select "Direct Deposit" and follow the prompts to enter your bank account and routing number.
  4. Save and confirm. Allow a pay cycle for the change to take effect. Contact cspay@umn.edu if you run into any issues.

Guidelines for Reporting Hours

Undergraduate TAs must report their hours consistently and accurately through MyU. Late reporting of hours can cause a delay in payment, and we want you to be paid on time. Log your hours as you go, don't wait until the end of the pay period.

  1. Go to myu.umn.edu and sign in with your UMN credentials.
  2. Select "My Time" from the navigation menu or your employee dashboard.
  3. Enter your hours for each day worked. Log the actual start and end time or total hours per task, as directed by your supervisor.
  4. Click "Submit Timesheet" before the pay period deadline. Deadlines vary — confirm the date with your supervisor or reach out to cscita@umn.edu if you are unsure.
Tip: Track your hours in real time using a notes app, calendar, or spreadsheet. Trying to remember a week's worth of work at the end of the pay period is easy to mess up and easy to undercount.

Examples of Work That Should Be Reported

Every course comes with different responsibilities, so this is not an exhaustive list. In general, if you are doing something because of your CSCI UG TA position, you should be paid for that time Just make sure your supervisor is aware and has approved it.

  • Labs, including arriving early to prepare and staying after to help students
  • Discussion sections and review sessions
  • Creating slides or materials for your lab or discussion
  • Office hours, including staying late if there is a student need
  • Grading, including time spent creating or reviewing rubrics
  • Working through homework or projects on your own to prepare to help students
  • Creating study guides, practice problems, or other student resources
  • Responding to student questions — email, Discord, Piazza, Slack, Canvas, etc.
  • Proctoring exams when assigned or volunteered
  • Weekly course staff meetings
  • Maintaining or updating the course website
  • Completing required trainings, such as the Implicit Bias training

Additional Guidance

Please work closely with your supervisor to make sure that the amount of work you are doing meets their expectations. If you would like to do additional work that was not assigned to you, such as more office hours or extra grading, communicate with your supervisor first to make sure that is okay.

We hired you as a UG TA, not as a volunteer. We want to make sure you are being paid for the work you do. If you have payroll questions, contact cspay@umn.edu.
